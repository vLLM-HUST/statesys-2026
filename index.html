Why StateSys

State has become the critical systems resource.

Modern services increasingly derive performance, efficiency, and service quality from how they organize, access, schedule, evolve, migrate, and reuse shared or long-lived state. StateSys 2026 brings these conversations together across cloud runtimes, data systems, storage stacks, serverless platforms, stream processing, graph systems, and AI serving infrastructure.

Call for Papers

Original research, system experience, position papers, and emerging ideas.

We invite submissions related to state management as a first-class systems problem. We especially welcome work that bridges foundational systems questions with emerging production settings such as large-model inference serving, retrieval-augmented services, and memory-centric cloud systems.

Topics

Systems problems where state is the center of gravity.

State Access

Modeling, observability, bottleneck diagnosis, locality, and topology-aware execution.

Runtime Control

Scheduling, concurrency control, recovery, consistency, elasticity, and reconfiguration.

Memory Hierarchies

KV/state placement, tiered caching, cache reuse, and cross-layer optimization.

State Evolution

Online updates, long-lived maintenance, migration, and reuse across requests or tasks.

AI Infrastructure

LLM serving, KV cache reuse, retrieval-augmented systems, and memory-centric inference.

Methodology

Benchmarks, workload characterization, evaluation practice, and reusable artifacts.

Program

A discussion-forward half-day workshop.

  1. Keynote

    Invited talk from a leading researcher or practitioner in stateful systems.

  2. Paper Session

    Selected research talks and experience reports from academia and industry.

  3. Panel

    Moderated discussion on state as a unifying abstraction across systems domains.

  4. Closing Forum

    Open benchmarks, interfaces, artifacts, and future community directions.
